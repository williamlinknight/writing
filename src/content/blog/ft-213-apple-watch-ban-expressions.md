## 为什么要读这篇？

你的学生是否在写"禁止""限制""法规"类话题时，只会用 `don't allow` 和 `stop someone from doing`？

今天这篇FT文章报道的是苹果因专利纠纷被禁止在美国销售新款手表——虽然话题涉及知识产权和技术法规，但其中的**因果链描述**（某行为 → 引发某裁决 → 导致某结果 → 后续行动），正是中高考和雅思议论文最常用的逻辑骨架。

我们从这篇报道中提取5个**法规/禁令场景通用的高级表达**，让你的作文在"规则与后果"类话题中瞬间提升一个档次。

## FT原文精选

> Apple **is banned from selling** the Watch Series 9 and Watch Ultra 2 in the US after President Joe Biden's administration **refused to grant a reprieve from** a trade tribunal's decision that it had infringed another company's patents.
>
> Apple confirmed on Tuesday it had **appealed against** the earlier ruling from the US International Trade Commission, which **stems from** a patent dispute with health technology company Masimo.
>
> The authority to decide whether to intervene in the dispute **was delegated by** the White House **to** the US trade representative, Katherine Tai.

## 5个考场高分表达

### 1. be banned from doing after [authority] refused to grant a reprieve


**原文出处：** "Apple is banned from selling the Watch…after…the administration refused to grant a reprieve from a trade tribunal's decision."

**为什么好：** 大多数学生写"禁止"只会用 `don't allow`。`be banned from doing` 更正式、更有力——而且这里加上了 `after [authority] refused to grant a reprieve` 这一**原因链条**，让禁令不再是一个孤立的动作，而是一连串事件的结果。这是高分写作最需要的"因果深度"。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The government doesn't allow people to use plastic bags. | Plastic bags **were banned after** major retailers **refused to grant a reprieve from** the new regulation. |
| The school stopped students from using phones. | Students **were banned from** using phones in class **after** repeated warnings. |

> **例句：** The city **banned** ride-hailing services **after** the local transport authority **refused to grant a reprieve from** safety regulations, citing multiple accidents.

---

### 2. refuse to grant a reprieve from 拒绝给予……的豁免

**原文出处：** "…refused to grant a reprieve from a trade tribunal's decision…"

**为什么好：** `reprieve`（缓刑/豁免）是一个极好的高级词汇。`refuse to grant a reprieve from X` 表达的是"不给X开绿灯""不让X免于制裁"。相比简单的 `refuse to allow`，这个搭配更有法律感和权威感。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The government didn't make any exception for the company. | The government **refused to grant a reprieve from** the new tax rules for big corporations. |
| The teacher didn't give him a second chance. | The school **refused to grant a reprieve from** the disciplinary action. |

> **例句：** Despite pleas from environmental groups, the court **refused to grant a reprieve from** the ban on single-use plastics, insisting it take full effect immediately.

---

### 3. stems from 源于 / 由……引起

**原文出处：** "…the earlier ruling from the US International Trade Commission, which stems from a patent dispute with health technology company Masimo."

**为什么好：** 写因果关系时，90%的学生只会用 `because` 和 `so`。`stems from` 是正式的因果表达，适合放在议论文中解释**一个决策或政策背后的根源**。它比 `comes from` 更书面，比 `results from` 更自然。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The problem is because of the new policy. | The controversy **stems from** the government's decision to raise taxes. |
| Many students feel stressed because of exams. | Students' anxiety **stems from** the overwhelming pressure to achieve high scores. |

> **例句：** The growing public distrust of social media platforms **stems from** repeated data breaches and the spread of misinformation.

---

### 4. appeal against [a decision/ruling] 对……提起上诉 / 不服……提出申诉

**原文出处：** "Apple confirmed on Tuesday it had appealed against the earlier ruling from the US International Trade Commission…"

**为什么好：** 在议论文中反驳对方观点时，多数学生只会写 `I disagree` 或 `Some people think this is wrong`。`appeal against` 不仅是法律用语，也是一种**有力的反驳结构**——表示"不接受当前结论，要求重新审视"。用于论证的"应对"环节非常出彩。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Many people disagree with this decision. | Many stakeholders **appealed against** the decision, arguing it would harm small businesses. |
| Some students think the rule is unfair. | Student representatives **appealed against** the new dress code, calling it outdated. |

> **例句：** Local residents **appealed against** the construction plan, claiming it would destroy the historic character of the neighbourhood.

---

### 5. the authority to [do] was delegated to [someone] ……被授权做某事

**原文出处：** "The authority to decide whether to intervene in the dispute was delegated by the White House to the US trade representative…"

**为什么好：** 这是一个极具正式感的被动结构。写作中涉及"某人被授权做某事""决定权被交给谁"时，`authority...was delegated to` 比 `someone was allowed to` 高级得多。被动语态加上抽象名词 `authority`，一步提升学术感。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The mayor can decide this issue. | The **authority** to approve the project **was delegated to** the mayor. |
| The teacher allows the students to choose. | The **authority** to set their own study schedules **was delegated to** senior students. |

> **例句：** The **authority** to regulate online content **was delegated to** an independent commission, which was tasked with balancing free speech and public safety.

## 写作建议：法规/禁令类话题的"四层因果链"

这篇FT文章最值得学习的是其**清晰的因果递进结构**。当你在考试中遇到"规则""禁令""政策"类话题（如是否应该禁止手机进校园、是否应该限制塑料使用），可以套用这个四层结构：

| 层次 | 功能 | 例句 |
|------|------|------|
| ① 禁令/Rule | 行为被禁止 | Cell phones **are banned from** classrooms. |
| ② 豁免/Reprieve | 是否有例外 | The school **refused to grant a reprieve from** the ban. |
| ③ 根源/Stem | 为什么 | The policy **stems from** concerns over student distraction. |
| ④ 反制/Appeal | 是否服气 | Some parents **appealed against** the decision. |

下次写"该不该禁止XX"时，不要在第一段就表态。先用这个四层因果链把问题说清楚——读者（考官）自然会觉得你思考有深度。
