你有没有过这样的经历？写议论文时，想表达「让步」「反对」「利用漏洞」这些概念，翻来覆去只会用 `give up`、`strongly disagree`、`take advantage of` —— 词没错，但太平了。

今天我们从《金融时报》一篇关于拜登基建谈判的报道中，精选 **6 个高频且实用的表达**。每一个都对应一个常见的写作场景，学了就能直接用。

---

## FT原文段落

> Joe Biden is prepared to **drop his demands** for an increase in the US corporation tax rate if enough Republicans agree to support a surge in infrastructure spending, according to people familiar with the negotiations.
>
> The concession by the US president is aimed at **catalysing** a bipartisan agreement on his bid to fund more than $1tn in additional spending.
>
> Republicans are **adamantly opposed** to it on the grounds that it would hurt the economy and partly **unwind** Donald Trump's signature tax cuts.
>
> He wants to impose a 15 per cent minimum tax on businesses so they cannot take advantage of **loopholes** that mean they end up paying very little or nothing at all.
>
> "This does not mean he is **jettisoning** his overall positions," said one person familiar with the talks.

---

## 1. drop one's demands — 放弃要求

**原文：** Biden is prepared to **drop his demands** for an increase in the US corporation tax rate.

**中文释义：** 放弃要求、不再坚持

**为什么好：**
大多数学生写「give up」。「Drop demands」是外交/谈判场景的标准用法，比「give up」更正式、更地道。

**❌ 学生初版**
> The government gave up its plan to increase taxes.

**✅ 升级版**
> The government dropped its demands for an immediate tax increase after facing strong opposition from businesses.

**💡 效果**
从口语化的「give up」升级为正式文体的「drop demands」，语气更沉稳。

**例句：**
- The school dropped its demands for mandatory Saturday classes after parents protested.
- The company dropped its demands for a full refund and agreed to a partial compensation instead.

---

## 2. catalyse — 催化、推动

**原文：** The concession by the US president is aimed at **catalysing** a bipartisan agreement.

**中文释义：** 催化、推动（一个过程/结果的发生）

**为什么好：**
学生常用 `cause` 或 `lead to`，这两个词太宽泛。`Catalyse` 源自化学术语"催化剂"，用在议论文中表示「推动某事加速发生」，非常精准有力。

**❌ 学生初版**
> The new policy caused economic growth.

**✅ 升级版**
> The government's investment in renewable energy catalysed a boom in green jobs across the country.

**💡 效果**
比「caused」更主动、更形象——像是在描述一种化学反应式的连锁反应。

**例句：**
- The teacher's encouragement catalysed a dramatic improvement in the student's writing performance.
- Social media catalysed the spread of awareness about environmental issues among young people.

---

## 3. adamantly opposed — 坚决反对

**原文：** Republicans are **adamantly opposed** to it on the grounds that it would hurt the economy.

**中文释义：** 坚决反对、毫不妥协地反对

**为什么好：**
`strongly disagree` 太口语化，`oppose` 太干。`Adamantly opposed` 是议论文中表达「强烈反对」的黄金搭配。

**❌ 学生初版**
> Many people strongly disagree with the new law.

**✅ 升级版**
> Many residents are adamantly opposed to the construction of a new factory near their homes, citing concerns over air pollution.

**💡 效果**
语气从「不同意」升级为「坚决反对」，体现议论文中的立场鲜明度。

**例句：**
- Environmental groups are adamantly opposed to the plan to drill for oil in protected areas.
- The student union was adamantly opposed to the proposed increase in tuition fees.

---

## 4. unwind — 撤销、松开

**原文：** ...partly **unwind** Donald Trump's signature tax cuts.

**中文释义：** 撤销（先前的政策/安排）；松开、解开

**为什么好：**
`unwind` 本义是「解开缠绕的东西」，用在政策语境中表示「撤销之前的举措」。比 `cancel` 或 `reverse` 更形象、更地道。

**❌ 学生初版**
> The new government canceled the previous policies.

**✅ 升级版**
> The new administration moved quickly to unwind several of its predecessor's controversial economic policies.

**💡 效果**
一个词就把「推翻前任政策」这个意思说清楚了，而且有一种「解绳结」的画面感。

**例句：**
- It took years to unwind the complex regulations that had been built up over the previous decade.
- The company decided to unwind its investment in the struggling startup.

---

## 5. loophole — 漏洞

**原文：** ...they cannot take advantage of **loopholes** that mean they end up paying very little or nothing at all.

**中文释义：** （法律、合同、税制中的）漏洞、空子

**为什么好：**
议论文中经常需要讨论「漏洞」这个概念——法律漏洞、制度漏洞、规则漏洞。`Loophole` 是标准用词，搭配 `exploit` 或 `take advantage of` 使用。

**❌ 学生初版**
> Some companies find problems in the law to avoid taxes.

**✅ 升级版**
> Some large corporations exploit legal loopholes to avoid paying their fair share of taxes.

**💡 效果**
从模糊的「problems in the law」升级为精准的「legal loopholes」，专业感立刻提升。

**例句：**
- The new regulation was designed to close the loopholes that had allowed excessive pollution.
- Many students try to find loopholes in the school rules to justify their behavior.

---

## 6. jettison — 抛弃、放弃

**原文：** "This does not mean he is **jettisoning** his overall positions..."

**中文释义：** 抛弃、丢弃（原计划/立场等）

**为什么好：**
`Jettison` 本义是「（船上）抛弃货物以减轻重量」，用在抽象语境中表示「果断放弃某个立场」。比 `abandon` 更生动、更有画面感。

**❌ 学生初版**
> The government abandoned its plan to reform the education system.

**✅ 升级版**
> The government chose not to jettison its ambitious education reform plan, despite fierce criticism from the media.

**💡 效果**
一个词就传递出「经过权衡后的战略性放弃」这种微妙含义。

**例句：**
- Rather than jettisoning their environmental commitments, the company found a more cost-effective way to achieve them.
- Many students jettison their hobbies in the final year of high school to focus entirely on exam preparation.

## 写作建议

这 6 个表达分别对应不同写作场景：

| 表达 | 适用场景 |
|------|---------|
| drop demands | 让步、放弃要求 |
| catalyse | 推动、引发变化 |
| adamantly opposed | 表达强烈反对 |
| unwind | 撤销政策/安排 |
| loophole | 讨论制度漏洞 |
| jettison | 战略性放弃 |

**练习方法：**
- 每天选一个词，写 3 个不同话题的句子
- 一周后，尝试在一篇完整的作文中自然融入 2-3 个
- 写作时不要强塞——用得自然比用得华丽更重要
