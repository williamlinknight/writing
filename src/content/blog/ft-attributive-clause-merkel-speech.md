很多同学的定语从句永远停留在"which is/that is"的初级阶段——整篇文章看下来，全是**The problem which is serious...**, **The thing that is important...** 的固定套路。

今天我们从FT对默克尔2020年讲话的报道中，挖出三个定语从句的高级用法，让你的从句写作直接从"初中水平"跳到"雅思7分"。

## FT原文段落

> Angela Merkel said Germany must go into a hard lockdown before Christmas to curb the spread of coronavirus, citing the "alarming" number of people being treated for Covid-19 in German intensive care wards **and the surge in deaths from the disease**.
>
> Ms Merkel said she accepted the recommendations of the Leopoldina, Germany's national academy of sciences, **which said this week** that all shops should be shut between December 24 and January 10.
>
> France is also struggling to control its "second wave" of coronavirus infections, **in spite of a nationwide lockdown that was due to be further eased next week**.
>
> Germany was widely seen to have coped much better with the pandemic's first wave than many of its neighbours, **with relatively low infection rates achieved without resort to draconian curfews and business closures**.

**中文对照：**

> 默克尔表示，德国必须在圣诞节前实行严格的封锁……理由是在德国重症监护病房接受治疗的新冠患者数量"惊人"。
>
> 默克尔表示，她接受了德国国家科学院——利奥波第那科学院(Leopoldina)的建议，**该机构本周表示**所有商店应在12月24日至1月10日期间关闭。
>
> 法国也在努力控制其"第二波"新冠病毒感染，**尽管全国范围的封锁将在下周进一步放松**。
>
> 人们普遍认为，德国应对第一波疫情的举措较其许多邻国有效得多，**在未实行严厉宵禁和要求企业停业的情况下，实现了相对较低的感染率**。

## 技巧一：非限制性定语从句补充背景信息

### 原文出處

> the Leopoldina, Germany's national academy of sciences, **which said this week that all shops should be shut...**

### 为什么好

大多数同学知道"带逗号的which从句"，但只会写最简单的信息——**the book, which I bought yesterday, is interesting**。FT的用法告诉我们：非限制性定语从句可以用来交代**机构的行动/观点**，而不只是"描述属性"。

这里`which said this week that...`交代的不是"科学院是什么"，而是**科学院做了什么**——这让句子信息密度翻倍。

**❌ 学生初版**
The Leopoldina is Germany's national academy of sciences. It said this week that all shops should be shut.

**✅ 升级版**
Ms Merkel accepted the recommendations of the Leopoldina, **which said this week that all shops should be shut...**

**💡 效果**
两个句子合并成一个，逻辑关系更紧密。"科学院"和"它说了什么"融为一体，读起来像母语者写的。

### 模板句式
```
[人物/机构], [职位/性质], which [动宾结构], [主句剩余部分]
```
> Prof. Smith, Harvard's leading epidemiologist, **which has published over 200 papers on the subject**, warned that the new variant could be more transmissible.

## 技巧二：用"with + 名词 + 过去分词"替代定语从句

### 原文出处

> ...with relatively low infection rates **achieved without resort to draconian curfews and business closures**

### 为什么好

这句话如果写成定语从句：**...with relatively low infection rates which were achieved without resort to...**——虽然语法正确，但冗长拖沓。

FT用的`achieved without resort to`是**过去分词作定语**（相当于`which were achieved`的省略形式），句子更紧凑有力。这是母语新闻写作的标志性技巧。

**❌ 学生初版**
Germany had low infection rates **which were achieved** without strict lockdowns.

**✅ 升级版**
Germany had relatively low infection rates **achieved without resort to** draconian curfews.

**💡 效果**
三个词（which were）变成零个词，信息密度瞬间提升。"achieved without resort to"连用的搭配也更高端，远超学生常用的"done without"。

### 模板句式
```
with [名词] + [过去分词] + [方式/条件]
```
> The city rebuilt its economy **with impressive growth achieved** through innovation rather than subsidies.

## 技巧三："in spite of + 名词 + that从句"的对比结构

### 原文出处

> ...in spite of a nationwide lockdown **that was due to be further eased next week**

### 为什么好

"in spite of/despite + 名词"是常见的让步结构，但同学们往往写到名词就停了——**in spite of the lockdown**。FT在这里加了`that was due to be further eased`，用定语从句交代了**这个lockdown的状态**，让让步对比更有力：尽管封锁即将放松（好趋势），但疫情仍然严重（坏情况）。

**❌ 学生初版**
France was still struggling. **Despite the lockdown**, the situation was bad.

**✅ 升级版**
France is also struggling **in spite of a nationwide lockdown that was due to be further eased next week**.

**💡 效果**
让步的对比感更强了——"锁国"和"即将放松"两层信息放在一起，衬托出问题的严峻。这是高级写作中"压缩信息密度"的核心技巧。

### 模板句式
```
[主句], in spite of / despite [名词] + that/which [补充状态]
```
> The company continued to lose money **despite a new strategy that was supposed to turn things around within months**.

## 写作建议

定语从句的精髓不在于会用`which/that/who`，而在于**用从句来交代背景、状态、原因**，而不是单纯描述属性。

下次写作文时，做完一个陈述句后问自己：**这个名词还能加什么背景信息？**——把那层信息塞进定语从句，你的文章立刻变厚。
