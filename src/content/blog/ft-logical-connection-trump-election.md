很多学生写作文的时候，只会用 **"but"** 从头连到尾。But this, but that——读起来像小学生吵架，而不是一篇有逻辑深度的议论文。

真正的转折和让步，在英文写作中是一整套武器库：**while、even after、just as...、in spite of、despite**。它们不仅能连接两个句子，还能在同一个句子里制造出"进退有度"的论证层次。

今天我们从《金融时报》一篇关于特朗普挑战2020年大选结果的文章中，学习4个高级转折/让步句式。

---

## FT原文示范

> Donald Trump's legal options to challenge his election loss **are about to significantly narrow**, with three battleground states carried by Joe Biden due to certify their results in the wake of a crushing weekend courtroom defeat for the Trump campaign.

> The certifications will arrive **just as** steadfast Republican support for Mr Trump's legal challenges **has begun to crack**.

> **But** Mr Trump has shown little sign that he will ever admit defeat, **even after** a conservative judge in Pennsylvania on Saturday evening tossed out a Trump campaign election lawsuit.

> Kevin Cramer ... defended Mr Trump for merely "exercising his legal options" **but** said it was "past time to start a transition".

---

## 四个逻辑衔接技巧

### 技巧一：`be about to` + `, with` —— 即将发生 + 伴随说明

**原文：**
> Trump's legal options **are about to significantly narrow**, **with** three battleground states due to certify their results.

**为什么好？** "be about to" 比 "will" 更有紧迫感，暗示"马上就要发生"。后面用 `, with + noun phrase` 说明伴随背景，不用写一个完整的从句。

**❌ 学生初版**
> Trump will lose his legal options. Three states will certify their votes.

**✅ 升级版**
> Trump's legal options are about to significantly narrow, with three battleground states due to certify their results.

**💡 效果**
"be about to" 比 "will" 更鲜活；", with" 把两句话压缩成一句话，行文更紧凑。适用于任何"事件即将发生+伴随背景"的场景。

**例句：** China's tourism industry is about to experience a major recovery, with millions of出境 travellers ready to book their first overseas trips.

---

### 技巧二：`just as X, Y` —— 两件事同步发生，形成张力

**原文：**
> The certifications will arrive **just as** steadfast Republican support for Mr Trump's legal challenges **has begun to crack**.

**为什么好？** "just as" 表示两件事几乎同时发生，制造出"倒计时感+变化正在发生"的双重效果。比 "at the same time" 更精准、更有画面感。

**❌ 学生初版**
> The certifications are coming. At the same time, Republican support is weakening.

**✅ 升级版**
> The certifications will arrive just as Republican support for Trump's legal challenges has begun to crack.

**💡 效果**
"just as" 把两件独立的事绑定在一起，暗示之间存在因果关系或逻辑联系。

**例句：** The final exam results are about to be released, just as students have begun to worry about their university applications.

---

### 技巧三：`But...even after...` —— 让步递进

**原文：**
> **But** Mr Trump has shown little sign that he will ever admit defeat, **even after** a conservative judge tossed out a Trump campaign election lawsuit.

**为什么好？** 这里用了两层转折：第一层是 **But**（与前文形成大转折），第二层是 **even after**（在转折内部再加一层"让步"——按理说法官驳回了就应该认输，但他没有）。这种"转折嵌套让步"是高级写作的标志。

**❌ 学生初版**
> Trump didn't admit defeat. A judge dismissed his lawsuit.

**✅ 升级版**
> But Mr Trump has shown little sign that he will ever admit defeat, even after a conservative judge tossed out his election lawsuit.

**💡 效果**
"even after" 比 "although" 更强——它不仅表达让步，还强调"时间上都过去了，但结果依然不变"。

**例句：** But the government has shown little sign that it will reverse the policy, even after thousands of protesters took to the streets.

---

### 技巧四：`, but` 小转折——同一主语下的对比

**原文：**
> Kevin Cramer defended Mr Trump ... **but** said it was "past time to start a transition".

**为什么好？** 这个 ", but" 不是连接两个句子，而是连接同一个人的两个行动——"defended" 和 "said" 之间的反差。这种"同一人同一句内的转折"比跨句转折更紧凑。

**❌ 学生初版**
> Kevin Cramer defended Trump. However, he said it was time to start a transition.

**✅ 升级版**
> Kevin Cramer defended Mr Trump for merely "exercising his legal options" but said it was "past time to start a transition".

**💡 效果**
不用断开句子，用 `, but` 把对比写在一句话里，人物立场显得更立体、更真实。

**例句：** The professor praised the student's creativity but noted that the argument lacked sufficient evidence.

---

## 写作建议

英文写作中的逻辑衔接，本质上是"让读者不费力地跟上你的思路"。今天学的四个技巧各有用途：

| 技巧 | 适用场景 |
|------|---------|
| `be about to` + `, with` | 背景铺垫，即将发生+伴随说明 |
| `just as X, Y` | 强调两件事同时发生，形成因果暗示 |
| `But ... even after ...` | 强烈对比，强调意外结果 |
| `, but` 同一主语内转折 | 让同一个人物的立场变得立体 |

下一次你写议论文的时候，试着用这些结构替换掉千篇一律的 "but" 和 "however"——你的作文会立刻上一个台阶。
