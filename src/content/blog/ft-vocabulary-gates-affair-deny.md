## 为什么你的作文总在"入门级"徘徊？

翻开一篇学生的议论文，最常见的"老三样"是：

> *He started a new project. The company looked into the problem. The meeting ended well.*

意思没错，但分数就是上不去。原因很简单：**你只用了最基础层的词汇，而评分标准里的"词汇资源"那一栏，看的是你能不能驾驭更精准、更正式的表达。**

来看一段《金融时报》的真实报道，感受一下顶级英文媒体的用词力量——

## 原文段落

> Microsoft in 2019 began to review a "concern" about a relationship between Gates, its co-founder and former chief executive, and a company employee 20 years earlier.
>
> "Microsoft received a concern in the latter half of 2019 that Bill Gates **sought to initiate** an intimate relationship with a company employee in the year 2000," Microsoft said in a statement. "A committee of the board reviewed the concern, **aided by an outside law firm to conduct a thorough investigation**."
>
> The billionaire said in March 2020 that he would **step down from** Microsoft's board to **dedicate more time to** philanthropy.
>
> "There was an affair almost 20 years ago which **ended amicably**," a spokesperson for Gates said in a statement.

## 为什么好？

FT记者在写一篇严肃的商业报道时，用了5个精准的高级词汇来替代日常口语化表达。每个词都不是"生僻词"，而是**议论文和正式书信中高频出现的"学术级表达"**。

我们逐一拆解：

---

## 1. initiate — 比 start 更正式、更主动

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| start a conversation | **initiate** a conversation |
| start a project | **initiate** a project / **launch** an initiative |
| start a relationship | **initiate** a relationship / **enter into** a relationship |

**为什么好：** `initiate` 带有"主动发起、正式开启"的含义，在学术写作和商务英语中出现频率是 `start` 的3倍以上。它告诉阅卷人：这个学生知道正式场合用什么词。

**模板：**
> [Subject] sought to initiate [something formal: a discussion / an investigation / a partnership].

**例句：**
> The government **sought to initiate** a public discussion on climate policy.
> 政府主动发起了关于气候政策的公众讨论。

---

## 2. alleged — 表达"未经证实的说法"的神器

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| something someone said | **alleged** misconduct / the **alleged** affair |
| they said he did it | his **alleged** approaches to colleagues |
| a claim that might be true | an **allegation** (n.) of wrongdoing |

**为什么好：** 写作中经常需要讨论"未证实的信息"。初学者写 `some people said that...`，而高级学习者用 `alleged` 一词就完成了"转述+保持客观"两个任务。这是新闻体、议论文、法律文书的高频词。

**模板：**
> There were **allegations** that [subject] [did something]. / [Subject] denied the **alleged** [noun].

**例句：**
> The CEO denied **allegations** of financial misconduct.
> CEO否认了关于财务不当行为的指控。

---

## 3. conduct an investigation — "做调查"的标准体

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| look into the problem | **conduct an investigation** into the matter |
| check what happened | **carry out / launch / conduct** an investigation |
| find out the truth | undertake a **thorough investigation** |

**为什么好：** `conduct an investigation` 是所有正式文体的固定搭配。加上 `thorough`（彻底的）之后，语气更严谨，表达更有分量。

**模板：**
> [Organization] **conducted a thorough investigation** into [issue], **aided by** [external party].

**例句：**
> The school **conducted a thorough investigation** into the cheating incident.
> 学校对作弊事件进行了彻底的调查。

---

## 4. dedicate...to — 比 spend time on 高级三个档次

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| spend more time on | **dedicate** more time **to** |
| use his time for | **devote** himself **to** / be **committed to** |
| focus on | **prioritize** |

**为什么好：** `dedicate to` 带有"全身心投入、郑重承诺"的意味。用在议论文中写一个人对事业/公益的投入，远比 `spend time on` 有说服力。

**模板：**
> [Subject] **dedicated** himself/herself **to** [cause / pursuit / goal], [purpose].

**例句：**
> After retiring, he **dedicated** himself **to** improving rural education.
> 退休后，他全身心投入改善乡村教育。

---

## 5. ended amicably — 高级副词的用法

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| ended well / ended peacefully | **ended amicably** |
| they separated nicely | the matter was resolved **amicably** |
| they agreed | they reached an **amicable** agreement (adj.) |

**为什么好：** `amicably` 是不太常见但非常高级的副词。它告诉阅卷人：这个学生会用副词来精确修饰动作，而不是每次都用 `well` 应付。

**模板：**
> The dispute **was resolved amicably** through [method].

**例句：**
> The two parties **resolved their differences amicably** without legal action.
> 双方在不诉诸法律的情况下友好地解决了分歧。

---

## 总结对照表

| 基础表达 | FT升级表达 | 适用场景 |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| start (v.) | **initiate** | 正式发起的动作（项目/讨论/调查） |
| said / claimed | **alleged** | 讨论未证实的说法 |
| look into (v.) | **conduct an investigation** | 正式的调查过程 |
| spend time on | **dedicate...to** | 投入/奉献/专注 |
| ended well | **ended amicably** | 友好地解决分歧 |

**行动建议：** 下次写议论文的时候，试着在三个地方用上今天学的词。不需要一次性全用——一篇作文里有2-3个高级词汇的精准使用，就足以让阅卷老师在"词汇资源"一栏给出高分了。
