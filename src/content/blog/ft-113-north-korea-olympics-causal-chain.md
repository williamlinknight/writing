## 为什么要读这篇？

写"一个决定导致连锁后果"时，学生是不是除了 `because` / `so` / `as a result` 就写不出别的了？

今天这篇FT报道讲的是朝鲜因新冠疫情退出东京奥运会，以及这一决定引发的**外交撤退、谈判僵局、关系恶化**——这是中高考和雅思常考的"因果关系""决策影响"类话题的绝佳素材。

最大的亮点是：作者用 **5种完全不同的句式** 来串联因果链，而不是机械重复 `because...so...`。学完这5种表达，你的因果段就能从"小学生逻辑"升级为"议论文逻辑"。

## FT原文精选

> North Korea **withdrew from** the Tokyo Olympics because of the coronavirus pandemic, **dashing** Seoul's hopes of using the event to improve inter-Korean relations and revive nuclear talks.
>
> The decision to withdraw coincides with deteriorating inter-Korean relations, which have been **put under additional strain** by North Korea's recent missile launches and a stalemate in nuclear talks between Washington and Pyongyang.
>
> The Olympics detente led to historic summits between Kim and then US president Donald Trump, where the North Korean leader pledged to denuclearise. **But talks have since stalled.** Kim **has cut engagement with** South Korean President Moon Jae-in.
>
> North Korea is the first country to withdraw from Tokyo 2020, **marking the latest setback for** a Games dogged by corruption allegations and the pandemic.

> **中文对照：** 朝鲜因新冠疫情退出东京奥运会，粉碎了韩国利用奥运会改善朝韩关系并重启核谈判的希望。这一决定正值朝韩关系恶化之际，朝鲜的导弹发射使关系更紧张。奥运会带来的缓和促成了金正恩与特朗普的会晤，但此后谈判陷入僵局。金正恩中断了与文在寅的接触。朝鲜是第一个退出东京奥运会的国家，标志着本届奥运会遭遇的最新挫折。

## 5个考场高分表达

### 1. withdraw from [event] + citing [reason] 以……为由退出……

**原文出处：** "North Korea withdrew from the Tokyo Olympics because of the coronavirus pandemic, citing the need to protect its athletes."

**为什么好：** 写"退出比赛/活动/协议"只会用 `leave` 或 `quit`？`withdraw from` 是书面语的标准表达，而 `citing [reason]` 更是一个**绝妙的"含因果的伴随状语"**——它把原因放在分词结构里，而不是另起一句写 `because`。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Many countries left the competition because of safety concerns. | Several teams **withdrew from** the tournament, **citing** safety concerns. |
| The company quit the project because costs were too high. | The company **withdrew from** the joint venture, **citing** rising operational costs. |

> **例句：** Several schools **withdrew from** the exchange programme, **citing** travel restrictions and health concerns.

---

### 2. dash one's hopes of [doing] 粉碎了……的希望

**原文出处：** "…dashing Seoul's hopes of using the event to improve inter-Korean relations and revive nuclear talks."

**为什么好：** 这是一个**及物分词结构**，前面是一个整件事（退出奥运会），后面紧跟着这件事的后果——"粉碎希望"。`dash` 在这里不是"猛冲"，而是"击碎"（像把杯子摔在地上一样）。比 `destroy one's hope` 更简洁、更有画面感。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The bad result destroyed our hope of winning. | The unexpected defeat **dashed** our hopes of **reaching** the finals. |
| The failure made us lose hope. | The budget cut **dashed** the team's hopes of **expanding** the research programme. |

> **例句：** The sudden cancellation of the scholarship programme **dashed** many students' hopes of **studying** abroad.

---

### 3. put [something] under (additional) strain 给……增加压力/使……更紧张

**原文出处：** "…which have been put under additional strain by North Korea's recent missile launches…"

**为什么好：** 写"让事情变糟"只会用 `make it worse`？`put [something] under strain` 表达的是**施加压力导致关系/系统处于紧绷状态**。`strain` 比 `pressure` 更强调"快要撑不住"的感觉。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The problem made the relationship worse. | The dispute **put** the trade relationship **under strain**. |
| The disagreement caused tension between them. | The territorial conflict **put** diplomatic relations **under considerable strain**. |

> **例句：** The increasing number of applications has **put** the university's admission system **under severe strain**.

---

### 4. talks have since stalled / has cut engagement with 谈判此后停滞 / 中断了与……的接触

**原文出处：** "But talks have since stalled. Kim has cut engagement with South Korean President…"

**为什么好：** 这两个表达放在一起效果极佳：`has since stalled` 用一个 `since` 隐含了**时间上的因果链**——从那以后就停滞了；`cut engagement with` 则用正式的"中断接触"替代了学生常写的 `stopped talking to`。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| After that, they stopped talking. | Official dialogue **has since stalled**, and both sides **have cut engagement with** each other. |
| The negotiations didn't continue. | The peace talks **have since stalled**, with neither side willing to compromise. |

> **例句：** The reform process **has since stalled** as the government **cut engagement with** opposition parties.

---

### 5. mark the latest setback for [something] 标志着……遭遇的最新挫折

**原文出处：** "…marking the latest setback for a Games dogged by corruption allegations…"

**为什么好：** 写"这是又一个坏消息"只会用 `This is another bad thing`？`mark the latest setback for` 是**结尾句的完美选择**：它在总结前文的同时，暗示前面还有一系列问题。`setback` 比 `failure` 更精准——它不是完全失败，而是挫折/倒退。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| This was another problem for the project. | The funding cut **marked the latest setback for** the ambitious energy plan. |
| This made things even harder for the team. | The star player's injury **marked the latest setback for** the already struggling team. |

> **例句：** The school's poor ranking in the national assessment **marked the latest setback for** the education reform programme.

---

## 总结

因果关系的表达力，决定了一篇议论文的档次。今天这5个表达告诉你一件事：**好的因果链不是堆砌 `because`，而是把原因和结果巧妙地藏在分词结构、名词短语和时间状语里。** 下次写"一个决定带来连锁反应"时，试试用 `withdraw...citing` 开头，用 `dashed hopes` 描述后果，中间穿插 `put under strain` 和 `has since stalled`，最后用 `marking the latest setback for` 收尾——这就是母语者的因果段写法。
