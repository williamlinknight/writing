## 为什么要读这篇？

写"压力""责任""辞职""危机"类作文时，学生是不是总在写 `leave the job`、`do something wrong`、`people are unhappy with`？

今天这篇FT报道讲的是哈佛大学第一任黑人校长克劳迪娜·盖伊（Claudine Gay）在短短6个月任期后辞职——话题涉及**领导力、学术诚信、公众舆论压力**。这不仅是国际新闻，更是中高考和雅思常考的"面对困难""承担责任"类话题的完美素材。

我们从报道中提取5个表达，让你的学生在写"危机与应对"时不再词穷。

## FT原文精选

> Claudine Gay **has resigned as** president of Harvard University **following** a widely criticised appearance at a congressional hearing on campus antisemitism that **prompted scrutiny of** plagiarism allegations against her.
>
> Gay was Harvard's first black president and **took office** only in July. Her **tenure** was the shortest in the university's history.
>
> In a statement, Gay said it had "become clear that **it is in the best interests of** Harvard for me to resign so that our community can **navigate this moment of extraordinary challenge** with a focus on the institution rather than any individual."

> **中文对照：** 克劳迪娜·盖伊辞去哈佛大学校长职务……此前她在一场关于校园反犹主义的国会听证会上受到广泛批评，引发了对她剽窃指控的审查。盖伊是哈佛首位黑人校长，去年7月才就任。她的任期是该大学历史上最短的。盖伊表示，很明显，"我辞职符合哈佛的最佳利益，这样我们的社区就可以在这个特殊挑战的时刻，把重点放在学校而不是任何个人身上。"

## 5个考场高分表达

### 1. resign as [position] following [event] 因……辞去……职务

**原文出处：** "Claudine Gay has resigned as president of Harvard following a widely criticised appearance at a congressional hearing…"

**为什么好：** 大多数学生写"辞职"只会用 `leave`。`resign as [position]` 是标准的正式表达，而 `following [event]` 则巧妙地交代了原因——**把因果关系隐含在时间状语里**，比 `because` 句更简洁、更高级。这是英语议论文中"不直说理由却暗示因果"的经典手法。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The CEO left the company because of the scandal. | The CEO **resigned as** the chairman **following** the accounting scandal. |
| The coach left his job after losing the game. | The coach **resigned as** head of the national team **following** a series of defeats. |

> **例句：** The Education Minister **resigned as** head of the reform committee **following** intense public backlash over the new examination system.

---

### 2. prompt scrutiny of [something] 引发对……的审查/关注

**原文出处：** "…a widely criticised appearance at a congressional hearing that prompted scrutiny of plagiarism allegations against her."

**为什么好：** 写"导致""引发"时，学生总是用 `lead to` 或 `cause`。`prompt scrutiny of` 表达的是"某事引起外界对某问题的严密关注/调查"——它比 `lead to investigation` 更精准，因为 `scrutiny` 本身就带有"仔细审视、不放过细节"的含义。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| This event led to people questioning his behavior. | The leaked document **prompted scrutiny of** the company's financial practices. |
| The problem caused many questions about school policy. | The cheating scandal **prompted scrutiny of** the school's examination procedures. |

> **例句：** The sudden rise in tuition fees **prompted scrutiny of** how universities allocate their funding, with many calling for greater transparency.

---

### 3. it is in the best interests of [X] for [someone] to do 做某事符合……的最佳利益

**原文出处：** "it had become clear that it is in the best interests of Harvard for me to resign…"

**为什么好：** 这是一句**气场极强的正式声明句式**。学生在写作中表达"为了大家好""最好的选择是……"时，往往写得琐碎且口语化。`it is in the best interests of X for someone to do` 不仅结构完整，而且自带"大局为重"的成熟感——适合用在议论文的结论段或建议段。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| It is better for everyone if they leave. | It is **in the best interests of** the team **for** the manager **to** step down. |
| We should do this for the good of all students. | It is **in the best interests of** all students **for** the school **to** adopt a more flexible curriculum. |

> **例句：** Many experts argue that **it is in the best interests of** the environment **for** governments **to** phase out fossil fuel subsidies immediately.

---

### 4. take office / tenure 就职 / 任期

**原文出处：** "Gay was Harvard's first black president and took office only in July. Her tenure was the shortest in the university's history."

**为什么好：** 写关于"领导人""任期""阶段"类话题时，多数学生用 `start the job`、`his time as` 等基本表达。`take office` 和 `tenure` 是一对黄金搭档：前者表示"就职"的正式说法，后者表示"在职期间"的专业术语。两个词放在一起，瞬间展现你的词汇深度。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The new principal started her job last year. | The new principal **took office** last year. |
| During his time as president, many changes happened. | During his **tenure** as president, significant reforms were implemented. |

> **例句：** The mayor **took office** at a time of economic crisis, and her **tenure** was marked by difficult but necessary budget cuts.

---

### 5. navigate a moment of [challenge/difficulty] 应对……时刻 / 度过难关

**原文出处：** "…so that our community can navigate this moment of extraordinary challenge with a focus on the institution rather than any individual."

**为什么好：** `navigate` 本意是"导航、驾驶船只"，引申为"妥善应对、有策略地度过难关"。相比之下，学生惯用的 `deal with` 和 `face` 显得干巴巴且缺乏方向感。`navigate a moment of X` 不仅语感高级，而且传达了一种"在复杂困境中保持方向感"的智慧——应用在议论文中写"如何应对挑战"时非常有质感。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The company has to deal with a difficult period. | The company must **navigate** a **moment of** financial difficulty. |
| Students should face challenges bravely. | Students learn to **navigate** **moments of** uncertainty and pressure. |

> **例句:** In times of rapid technological change, schools must help students **navigate** **moments of** uncertainty by teaching them adaptable skills rather than fixed knowledge.

## 写作建议：写"危机与选择"的经典三段结构

这篇报道其实讲述了一个完整的"危机决策"过程。当你的学生遇到"面对困难该如何选择"类话题时（如高考英语作文中的"一次困难抉择"、雅思大作文中的"危机管理"），可以借鉴这个三段结构：

| 段落 | 内容 | 例句参考 |
|------|------|---------|
| ① 起因 | 谁会因为什么事件被关注 | The CEO **resigned as** head of the company **following** an accounting scandal that **prompted scrutiny of** the firm's financial reporting. |
| ② 背景 | 该人物的背景和处境 | He **took office** only a year ago, and his short **tenure** was already marked by controversy. |
| ③ 选择与态度 | 以大局为重，做出艰难决定 | He stated that **it was in the best interests of** the company **for** him to step down, allowing the team to **navigate** this **moment of** crisis. |

这个结构的好处是：**不是简单地讲"发生了什么事"**，而是呈现了一个有前因、有背景、有态度的完整叙事——这正是高分作文和低分作文之间最本质的区别。
