## 为什么要读这篇？

雅思大作文的"环境与科技"类题目、高考的"利弊分析"类作文，核心难点从来不是词汇——而是**如何有层次地呈现争议双方的观点**。

今天这篇FT报道完美示范了这一点：日本政府决定将福岛核污水排入大海，环保主义者反对，渔民的愤怒，韩国的遗憾，美国的谨慎支持——**3个不同立场的反应，3种不同的表态方式**，全在一篇报道里。学会这5种论证表达，你的"争议话题"作文就不再是单方面的指责或辩护，而是有深度的正反分析。

## FT原文精选

> Japan will release more than 1m tonnes of contaminated water from the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant into the Pacific, **a move condemned by** environmentalists, fishermen and neighbouring countries.
>
> The decision **risked reviving** some of the trauma of the nuclear accident and **worsening** its legacy of pollution.
>
> But Japanese authorities argued that **there was no practical alternative** to releasing the water as storage space ran out. They added **there was no risk to** human health.
>
> The government **considered several alternatives**, including evaporating the water into the atmosphere or injecting it into underground reservoirs. But experts argued that releasing into the ocean was the only viable choice.
>
> South Korea expressed its "strong regret" but the US **cautiously endorsed** Japan's approach.

> **中文对照：** 日本将把福岛第一核电站超过100万吨受污染的水排进太平洋，此举遭到环保主义者、渔民和周边国家谴责。这一决定有可能重新揭开核事故创伤。但日本当局辩称没有替代方案，并表示对人类健康不构成风险。政府考虑了几种替代方案，但专家认为排海是唯一可行选择。韩国表示强烈遗憾，但美国谨慎支持日本的做法。

## 5个考场高分表达

### 1. a move [过去分词] by [群体] 一个遭到……的行动

**原文出处：** "Japan will release…into the Pacific, a move condemned by environmentalists, fishermen and neighbouring countries."

**为什么好：** 这是**同位语总结**的经典用法——先用一句话陈述事实/决策，然后加 `a move [过去分词] by…` 来展现各方反应。比常见的 `This action was criticized by…` 更简洁、更有层次。

`a move condemned by` — 遭到谴责
`a move welcomed by` — 受到欢迎
`a move questioned by` — 受到质疑

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The government decided to build a new factory. Many people criticized this decision. | The government approved the construction of a new factory, **a move criticized by** local residents and environmental groups. |
| The school changed the exam system. This was welcomed by students. | The school introduced a new assessment system, **a move welcomed by** most students and parents. |

> **例句：** The city announced a ban on single-use plastics in all restaurants, **a move applauded by** environmental advocates but questioned by small business owners.

---

### 2. risk [doing] / risk [noun] 冒着……的风险 / 有可能导致……

**原文出处：** "The decision risked reviving some of the trauma of the nuclear accident and worsening its legacy of pollution."

**为什么好：** `risk` 后接动词-ing 或名词，表达的是"这个决定可能引起负面后果"。很多学生用 `may cause` / `could lead to`，但 **`risk doing` 带有一个关键的语气差**：它暗示决策者明知道有风险还是做了，比 `may cause` 多了一层批判性。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| This plan may cause more pollution. | This plan **risks worsening** the already fragile environmental situation. |
| If they do this, public trust could be damaged. | The policy **risks eroding** public trust in the education system. |

> **例句：** Cutting funding for arts programmes **risks undermining** the development of students' creative thinking.

---

### 3. there is no practical alternative to [doing] 除此之外别无可行的选择

**原文出处：** "…authorities argued that there was no practical alternative to releasing the water as storage space ran out."

**为什么好：** 写"不得不做某事"时，学生最爱写 `have to do` 或 `there is no choice but to do`。`there is no practical alternative to doing` 更正式、更地道——`practical` 这个词是关键：不是"没有别的选择"，而是"没有**可行的**选择"，给论证留了转圜余地。

这个表达在议论文中通常作为**让步段的关键句**出现：先承认某事不好，再用这句话解释为什么还是做了。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| They had to raise taxes because there was no other choice. | The government argued that **there was no practical alternative to** raising taxes given the budget deficit. |
| The school had to cancel the trip. | Administrators maintained that **there was no practical alternative to** cancelling the overseas exchange programme. |

> **例句：** While critics condemned the tuition fee increase, the university insisted that **there was no practical alternative to** raising fees if it wanted to maintain teaching quality.

---

### 4. consider several alternatives, including… / dismiss [alternative] 考虑多种方案 / 否定了某些选项

**原文出处：** "The government considered several alternatives, including evaporating the water into the atmosphere or injecting it into underground reservoirs. But experts argued that…was the only viable choice."

**为什么好：** 写议论文时，**先说考虑了其他方案，再给出最终理由**，这是论证中的"全面性"手法——表明你不是一拍脑袋就做决定，而是权衡过的。`consider several alternatives` + `dismiss/argue against` 的组合比直接说 `we chose this because` 更有说服力。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| We chose this plan because it's the best. | The committee **considered several alternatives, including** extending the deadline and hiring temporary staff, but ultimately **dismissed** them as too costly. |
| This is the only way to solve the problem. | The group **considered several alternatives, including** reducing class sizes and introducing online courses, before settling on the hybrid model. |

> **例句：** The education authority **considered several alternatives, including** merging small schools and building new dormitories, but **dismissed** both options due to budget constraints.

---

### 5. cautiously endorse / express [strong] regret / voice objections 不同立场的表态方式

**原文出处：** 
- "South Korea expressed its 'strong regret'…"
- "the US cautiously endorsed Japan's approach"
- "We hereby register our strong objections"

**为什么好：** 写"各方反应"时，学生只知道 `agree` 和 `disagree`。但真实的写作中——尤其是雅思大作文——**立场的轻重和程度**决定了文章的深度：

| 立场程度 | 表达 |
|---------|------|
| 强烈反对 | **voice strong objections to / express strong regret over** |
| 担忧但不反对 | **express concern about** |
| 谨慎支持 | **cautiously endorse / cautiously welcome** |
| 完全支持 | **fully endorse / give full backing to** |

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Some people agreed with the plan, but others didn't. | Environmentalists **voiced strong objections to** the plan, while the US **cautiously endorsed** it. |
| Some students like the new rule, some don't. | Parent groups **expressed concern about** the new homework policy, though teachers **cautiously welcomed** it. |

> **例句：** While local residents **voiced strong objections to** the construction of the factory, the municipal government **cautiously endorsed** the project, citing job creation and economic growth.

## 总结

写争议话题的作文，最怕的是**只有立场，没有论证层次**。今天这5个表达教你一个三段式论证框架：

1. **陈述决策 + 各方反应**：用 `a move condemned/welcomed by…`
2. **解释为什么别无选择**：用 `there is no practical alternative to…` + `consider several alternatives, including…`
3. **展现不同立场**：用 `cautiously endorse` / `express regret` / `voice objections`

下次写"政府决策""科技争议""环保话题"类作文时，试试用这个框架——你的论证就不再是"我认为好/不好"，而是**有结构的、有层次的、有说服力的分析**。
