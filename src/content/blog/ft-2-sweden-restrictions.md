疫情期间，瑞典这个「欧洲抗疫特例」一度拒绝封锁，依靠公民自觉防疫。但随着病例激增，政府终于收紧限制。今天我们从这篇FT报道中提取5个百搭的高分表达——它们都不是疫情专用词，写教育、社会、科技类作文同样好用。

## 原文精选

> Sweden has **unveiled** tougher restrictions to fight coronavirus as Europe's outlier on Covid-19 is forced to step up measures to fight the virus as it faces surging numbers of cases and deaths.

> Sweden has **stood out from** the rest of Europe by refusing to order a legal lockdown and instead preferring to rely on recommendations.

> Most of its response has produced what its state epidemiologist described as a **de facto** lockdown.

> Mr Lofven **struck a solemn tone** on Monday, twice telling Swedes: "This is a **testing time**."

## 词汇解析

### 1️⃣ unveil — 推出、公布、揭晓

**原文出处：** "Sweden has **unveiled** tougher restrictions"

**为什么好：** 很多学生一提到「推出政策、公布措施」只会用 `announce`。`Unveil` 的原始意思是「揭开面纱」，用在正式语境里有一种「郑重其事地发布」的感觉，比 `announce` 更生动、更有仪式感。

**考场用法对比：**

**❌ 学生初版**
The government announced a new plan to reduce plastic waste.

**✅ 升级版**
The government **unveiled** a new plan to reduce plastic waste.

**💡 效果**
`Unveil` 比 `announce` 多了一层「首次公之于众」的意味。写政府措施、公司发布新产品时，`unveil` 比 `announce` 更能体现「重磅消息」的分量。

**例句：**
The company **unveiled** its latest smartphone model at the annual conference.

---

### 2️⃣ stand out from — 与…不同、脱颖而出

**原文出处：** "Sweden has **stood out from** the rest of Europe"

**为什么好：** 学生的作文里写「不同」通常是 `be different from`，写「优点」是 `be good at`。`Stand out from` 一个短语就包含了「与众不同 + 引人注目」两层意思。

**考场用法对比：**

**❌ 学生初版**
This school is different from other schools because of its teaching methods.

**✅ 升级版**
This school **stands out from** other schools for its unique teaching methods.

**💡 效果**
`Stand out from` 比 `be different from` 更主动、更有「优势感」。写学校、公司、个人优势时非常好用。后面接 `for`（原因）或 `by`（方式）。

**例句：**
What **stands out from** the report is the sharp increase in renewable energy investment.

---

### 3️⃣ de facto — 事实上的（非正式的）

**原文出处：** "produced what its state epidemiologist described as a **de facto** lockdown"

**为什么好：** 学生写「虽然是某种情况、但实际上是另一种」时，通常用长句子解释。`De facto` 是拉丁语外来词，一个词就表示「虽然没有正式承认，但事实上就是如此」。

**考场用法对比：**

**❌ 学生初版**
Although there was no official policy, students were actually required to wear uniforms.

**✅ 升级版**
There was a **de facto** uniform policy, even though it was never officially stated.

**💡 效果**
`De facto` 是学术写作的经典用法，尤其在议论文中讨论「名义 vs 实质」的差异时非常好用。比 `in fact`、`actually` 更精准、更正式。

**例句：**
The exam has become a **de facto** requirement for university admission, despite being officially optional.

---

### 4️⃣ strike a tone — 表现出某种语气/态度

**原文出处：** "Mr Lofven **struck a solemn tone**"

**为什么好：** 学生想表达「某人以某种态度说话」时，通常写 `he spoke seriously` 或 `he said in a serious way`。`Strike a tone` 是地道新闻英语的常用搭配，后面接形容词（serious, optimistic, cautious, defiant 等），准确又高级。

**考场用法对比：**

**❌ 学生初版**
The president spoke seriously about the climate crisis.

**✅ 升级版**
The president **struck a serious tone** when addressing the climate crisis.

**💡 效果**
`Strike a tone` 把「说话方式」变成一个可拆装的短语模板，注入不同形容词即可写出多样化的表达。

**例句：**
The CEO **struck an optimistic tone** about the company's future during the interview.

---

### 5️⃣ testing time — 考验时刻

**原文出处：** "This is a **testing time**. It is about your and my choices every day, every hour, every moment."

**为什么好：** 学生写「艰难时期」总写 `difficult time` 或 `hard time`。`Testing time` 强调的不是「困难本身」，而是「这个困难在考验我们的能力」，视角更积极。

**考场用法对比：**

**❌ 学生初版**
During this difficult time, we need to work hard.

**✅ 升级版**
We are going through a **testing time**, but it will make us stronger.

**💡 效果**
`Testing time` 暗示「困难是有意义的——它在检验我们的意志和能力」。用在作文结尾段，比 `difficult time` 更有力量、更有总结感。

**例句：**
The pandemic was a **testing time** for small businesses, but many adapted and survived.

## 写作建议

这5个表达有一个共同点：**它们都不是生僻词，而是用法的高级化。**

- `Unveil` 不是生词，但学生想不到用它代替 `announce`
- `Stand out from` 是初中词汇的组合，但学生不知道这个搭配
- `De facto` 简单到只有两个音节，但知道它用法的人很少
- `Strike a tone` 的每个词你都认识，但组合用法却想不到

这就是读外刊的意义——学的不是「新单词」，而是 **「旧单词的新组合」**。

下次写作文时，试着去掉 `announce`、`different`、`in fact` 这些平庸表达，换成今天学的词汇。一篇800词作文里有3-5个这样的地道表达，就已经是质的提升了。
