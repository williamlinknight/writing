## 为什么你写的句子总是不够"有力"？

批改作文时，我最常见的问题不是语法错误，而是"动词太弱"。

学生习惯用：**went down, made up for, caused...to, started to check, introduced**——这些词不是错，但它们太笼统、太口语化，像一杯白开水。高分写作的秘密就在于：**用一个精准的动词，代替一个模糊的短语。**

今天我们从FT一篇关于快手股价下跌的商业报道（原文603词）中，提取8个**一看就能直接用**的高分动词。

## 原文段落

> Shares in ByteDance's main competitor in China **tumbled** after the company's losses widened.
>
> The company's shares fell 11.5 per cent, **erasing** as much as 14bn off Kuaishou's market capitalisation.
>
> Kuaishou's short video app faces **stiffening** competition.
>
> The earnings figures **prompted** Wall Street banks including Morgan Stanley to cut their share price targets.
>
> Growth in the company's advertising business helped **offset** the decline.
>
> Regulators have also **rolled out** new rules to control livestreaming content.
>
> The company's results come as Beijing **steps up scrutiny** of the country's tech sector.
>
> Bernstein also pointed to Kuaishou's **ballooning** sales-and-marketing expenses.

> 译文：字节跳动在中国的主要竞争对手快手的股价**大幅下跌**。该公司股价下跌11.5%，**令**其市值**蒸发**多达140亿美元。快手的短视频应用面临**日益激烈**的竞争。这些业绩数据**促使**华尔街银行下调目标价。广告业务增长帮助**抵消**了收入下滑。监管机构也**出台了**新规。该公司业绩发布之际，中国政府正**加强**对科技行业的**审查**。伯恩斯坦还指出快手不断**膨胀**的销售和营销费用。

## 8个高分表达精讲

### 1. tumble — 不只是"fall"

**原文：** Shares...tumbled after the company's losses widened（股价在亏损扩大后大幅下跌）

**为什么好：** 学生写股价变化只会用 `went down` 或 `fell`。`tumble` 不仅表示下跌，还暗示**速度快、幅度大、出人意料**——一个词顶三个词。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The stock price **went down** quickly. | The stock price **tumbled**. |
| House prices **fell** sharply last year. | House prices **tumbled** last year. |

**公式：** `[Subject] tumbled [number%]`

**例句：** Tech stocks **tumbled** 8% after the government announced new regulations.

### 2. erase 蒸发/抹去

**原文：** erasing as much as 14bn off Kuaishou's market capitalisation（让市值蒸发多达140亿美元）

**为什么好：** 学生写"失去价值"只会用 `took away` 或 `lost`。`erase`（抹去）是更生动、更正式的动词，适合描述**价值的消失**。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The company **lost** a lot of value. | The crisis **erased** 2bn off the company's market value. |
| The disaster **took away** years of progress. | The disaster **erased** years of progress. |

**公式：** `[Event] erased [amount] off [value/noun]`

**例句：** A single data breach **erased** the company's entire quarterly profit.

### 3. offset — 抵消（不是make up for）

**原文：** Growth in advertising business helped **offset** the decline（广告业务增长帮助抵消了这轮下滑）

**为什么好：** 学生写"弥补/抵消"会用 `make up for`（口语化）或者 `balance`（不精准）。`offset` 是**正式写作的标准动词**，尤其在利弊分析类作文中极其好用。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The benefits **made up for** the costs. | The benefits **offset** the costs. |
| His effort **balanced** the team's weakness. | His effort **offset** the team's weakness. |

**公式：** `[Factor A] offset [Factor B]`

**例句：** The new teaching method's higher cost was **offset** by improved student outcomes.

### 4. prompt — 促使（不是cause...to）

**原文：** The earnings figures **prompted** Wall Street banks to cut their share price targets（这些业绩数据促使华尔街银行下调了快手目标股价）

**为什么好：** 学生写"导致/促使"就用 `made...do` 或 `caused...to`。`prompted...to` 更精准——它暗示**因为某个具体原因而采取了行动**。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The test results **made** the school **change** its policy. | The test results **prompted** the school **to change** its policy. |
| The report **caused** the government **to act**. | The report **prompted** the government **to act**. |

**公式：** `[Event] prompted [Entity] to [action]`

**例句：** Rising unemployment **prompted** the local government to launch a job-training program.

### 5. roll out — 正式推出

**原文：** Regulators have also **rolled out** new rules to control livestreaming content（监管机构也出台了新规）

**为什么好：** 学生写"出台/推出"就用 `made`、`started`、`introduced`。`roll out` 是**正式的动词短语**，尤其适合描述政策、规则、产品的正式发布。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The government **made** new rules. | The government **rolled out** new rules. |
| The school **started** a new program. | The school **rolled out** a new program. |

**公式：** `[Authority] rolled out [policy/program/rules]`

**例句：** The Ministry of Education **rolled out** a new curriculum for high schools across the country.

### 6. step up scrutiny — 加强审查

**原文：** Beijing **steps up scrutiny** of the country's tech sector（中国政府正加强审查国内科技行业）

**为什么好：** 学生写"加强检查/监管"会说 `check more` 或 `strengthen supervision`——翻译腔太重。`step up scrutiny` 是**地道的固定搭配**，`scrutiny` 是"严密审查"的意思，比 `check` 正式得多。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The government **started to check** online content more strictly. | The government **stepped up scrutiny** of online content. |
| Schools **must check** safety more carefully. | Schools **must step up scrutiny** of campus safety. |

**公式：** `[Authority] stepped up scrutiny of [field/area]`

**例句：** After the scandal, the university **stepped up scrutiny** of its admissions process.

### 7. ballooning — 急剧膨胀

**原文：** **ballooning** sales-and-marketing expenses（不断膨胀的销售和营销费用）

**为什么好：** 学生写"快速增加"只会 `quickly increasing`——又长又平淡。`ballooning` 来自"气球膨胀"的画面感，一个词就传达出**不受控制地快速变大**的含义。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The company's **quickly increasing** costs are worrying. | The company's **ballooning** costs are worrying. |
| The **growing** government debt has become a problem. | The **ballooning** government debt has become a problem. |

**公式：** `ballooning [costs/expenses/debt/budget]`

**例句：** The city is struggling with **ballooning** housing prices that young people can no longer afford.

### 8. stiffening — 日益激烈的

**原文：** faces **stiffening** competition（面临日益激烈的竞争）

**为什么好：** 学生写"越来越激烈"就说 `more and more fierce`。`stiffening` 一词搞定——它既有"变强"又有"变艰难"的含义，在商业和考试作文里极其高频。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The team faces **more and more fierce** competition. | The team faces **stiffening** competition. |
| There is **increasingly strong** opposition to the plan. | There is **stiffening** opposition to the plan. |

**公式：** `stiffening [competition/opposition/resistance]`

**例句：** Chinese students applying to top US universities face **stiffening** competition every year.

## 升级版段落对照

看看这些词汇如何把一个"学生段"变成"高分段"：

**学生版：**
> The company had problems because its costs **quickly increased**. The government also **started to check** the tech sector more strictly. This situation **made** investors **lose** confidence. The benefits of new products could not **make up for** the losses.

**升级版：**
> The company struggled as its costs **ballooned**. Meanwhile, the government **stepped up scrutiny** of the tech sector. This situation **prompted** investors to lose confidence. The benefits of new products could not **offset** the losses.

## 写作建议

这8个词有一个共同特点：**它们都替代了学生最常用的"安全词"。**

下次写完作文，做一件事：圈出你所有的 `made`、`went`、`had`、`did`，问自己——**有没有一个更精准的动词可以替换？**

不用一次全记住。先挑3个最顺手的（我建议 `prompt...to`、`offset`、`roll out`），练10篇，变成肌肉记忆，再加上新的。
