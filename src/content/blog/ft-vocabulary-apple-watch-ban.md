## 读《金融时报》学英文写作：从苹果手表专利战学会让作文高级的10组词汇

很多同学写英语作文时，明明想表达"停止""恢复""影响"这些意思，翻来覆去只会用 `stop`、`start`、`influence`。这些词没错，但读起来像小学生——尤其是在中考、高考和雅思的议论文里，正式语境需要更精准、更正式的表达。

今天我们从FT一篇关于苹果恢复销售手表（Apple Watch）的报道中，提取10组让作文立刻高级起来的词汇，附使用方法。

### 📰 FT原文节选

> **原文：** Apple is resuming sales in the US of its Watch Series 9 and Ultra 2 after an appeals court halted a ban handed down by a trade tribunal, the latest development in an ongoing patent battle with health technology company Masimo.
>
> **译文：** 在上诉法院暂停了贸易法庭下达的禁令后，苹果公司恢复在美国销售其Watch Series 9和Ultra 2——这是该公司与Masimo正在进行的专利战的最新进展。
>
> **原文：** Last week Apple had pulled the latest models of its watch from its online and physical stores in the US in anticipation of the ban from the International Trade Commission taking effect. On Wednesday, after an appeals court granted its request to suspend the ban for now, Apple confirmed the products would be returning to both its online and physical stores.

### 🔑 10组词汇升级

#### 1. resume → 代替 start again / begin again

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The class will **start again** next week. | The class will **resume** next week. |
| They **began** the meeting after lunch. | They **resumed** the meeting after lunch. |

**模板：** `[Subject] resumed [noun] after [reason]`

> ✏️ 例句：The government **resumed** negotiations after the strike ended.

#### 2. halt / suspend → 代替 stop

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The project was **stopped** suddenly. | The project was **halted** suddenly. |
| The school **stopped** classes because of the storm. | The school **suspended** classes due to the storm. |

**模板：** `[Subject] halted/suspended [noun] in anticipation of [reason]`

> ✏️ 例句：The company **halted** production in **anticipation of** new regulations.

#### 3. in anticipation of → 代替 because / since

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| They stopped **because** the storm was coming. | They stopped **in anticipation of** the storm. |
| We prepared **because** the exam was coming. | We prepared **in anticipation of** the exam. |

**模板：** `in anticipation of [future event]`

> ✏️ 例句：Many countries raised interest rates **in anticipation of** inflation.

#### 4. take effect → 代替 start / begin（法律/规则生效）

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The new law **starts** next month. | The new law **takes effect** next month. |
| The policy began last week. | The policy **took effect** last week. |

**模板：** `[rule/law/regulation] takes effect on [date]`

> ✏️ 例句：The smoking ban **took effect** in public places in 2020.

#### 5. grant a request → 代替 agree / allow

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The judge **agreed** to their request. | The judge **granted** their request. |
| Our teacher **allowed** us to leave early. | Our teacher **granted** our request to leave early. |

**模板：** `[Authority] granted [someone]'s request to [do something]`

> ✏️ 例句：The committee **granted** the school's request to extend the deadline.

#### 6. confirm → 代替 say yes / make sure

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The school **said** the test is next week. | The school **confirmed** that the test is next week. |
| He **made sure** the meeting was still on. | He **confirmed** the meeting was still scheduled. |

**模板：** `[Subject] confirmed that [clause] / [Subject] confirmed [noun]`

> ✏️ 例句：The CEO **confirmed** that the company would expand into Asia.

#### 7. available for purchase → 代替 can be bought

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The new iPhone **can be bought** now. | The new iPhone is **available for purchase** now. |
| These tickets **can be bought** online. | These tickets are **available for purchase** online. |

**模板：** `[Product] will become available for purchase [time/place]`

> ✏️ 例句：The revised textbook will be **available for purchase** next semester.

#### 8. in a statement → 在对话/引语中的正式表达

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The company **said** "We are happy." | The company **said in a statement**: "We are happy." |
| The CEO **told** reporters he was excited. | The CEO **said in a statement** that he was excited. |

**模板：** `[Subject] said in a statement that [clause]`

> ✏️ 例句：The university **said in a statement that** it would increase scholarships.

#### 9. the latest development in → 代替 a new thing / the newest

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| This is a **new step** in the project. | This is the **latest development in** the project. |
| This is the **newest event** in this story. | This is the **latest development in** this story. |

**模板：** `the latest development in [ongoing situation]`

> ✏️ 例句：The tariff reduction is the **latest development in** trade relations.

#### 10. ongoing → 代替 continuing / still happening

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The **continuing** problem needs attention. | The **ongoing** problem needs attention. |
| The **still happening** debate is important. | The **ongoing** debate is important. |

**模板：** `ongoing [noun]`（ongoing永远是形容词，后面跟名词）

> ✏️ 例句：There is an **ongoing** discussion about climate change policy.

### 📝 学生版 vs 升级版段落对照

**原版（学生腔）：**
> The company stopped selling its watches because a ban was going to start. But later a court agreed to their request and let them sell again. The company said the watches could be bought again now.

**升级版（FT风格）：**
> The company halted sales of its watches in anticipation of a ban taking effect. However, after the court granted the company's request, it resumed sales. The company confirmed that the watches were available for purchase again — the latest development in an ongoing legal dispute.

### ✅ 今日小结

这10组词的核心逻辑是：**用具体、正式的单字动词，替代笼统、口语化的短语动词。**

中文学生最常见的写作问题之一就是"动词太弱"——总是用 `have`、`make`、`do`、`get` 加名词的组合。下次写作文时，试着用今天学习的词替换一两个，效果立竿见影。
