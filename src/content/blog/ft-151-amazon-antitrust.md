## 为什么要读这篇？

反垄断（antitrust）话题听起来很专业，但它报道逻辑非常清晰——**某方指控某方做了什么，造成了什么后果，双方各执一词**。这种论证结构，恰恰是中考、高考、雅思大作文最常用的逻辑框架。

今天从FT一篇关于华盛顿特区起诉亚马逊的报道中，提取5个**可直接搬进考场作文**的表达。

---

## FT原文精选

> Washington DC has **sued Amazon for allegedly violating** the district's competition laws by illegally tying merchants to restrictive selling rules.
>
> Karl Racine accused the ecommerce giant of charging third-party merchants fees of as much as 40 per cent of a product's price, and then requiring them not to charge less on other platforms.
>
> The new lawsuit claimed Amazon's "restrictive contracts" **artificially inflate** prices for consumers.
>
> "Amazon wins because it controls pricing across the online retail sales market, **putting itself at an advantage over** everyone else," he said.

> "The DC attorney-general **has it exactly backwards** — sellers set their own prices for the products they offer in our store," the company said in a statement.
>
> The case brought by Racine will **be seen as a test for** whether other states can build their own cases against Amazon.

> 中文对照：华盛顿特区起诉亚马逊涉嫌违反当地竞争法……新诉讼称亚马逊的"限制性合同"人为抬高了消费者价格……"亚马逊之所以成功是因为它控制了定价，使自己相对于其他平台处于优势。""哥伦比亚特区总检察长完全是本末倒置。"……此案将被视为其他州能否对亚马逊提起诉讼的一次考验。

## 5个考场高分表达

### 1. sue … for allegedly + V-ing 起诉某人涉嫌做某事

**原文出处：** "Washington DC has sued Amazon for allegedly violating the district's competition laws."

**为什么好：** 很多学生写"批评某人做某事"只会用 `criticize` 或 `blame`。`sue for allegedly doing` 是一个**法律-论证**语境的高频结构，用在议论文中能瞬间提升论证的正式度和说服力。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Many people criticize companies for polluting the environment. | Many people **sue** companies **for allegedly** polluting the environment. |
| Some say the government didn't do its job. | Some **sue** the government **for allegedly** failing to protect citizens' rights. |

> **例句：** Residents **sued** the factory **for allegedly** discharging toxic waste into the river, demanding compensation for health damage.

---

### 2. artificially inflate 人为抬高

**原文出处：** "The new lawsuit claimed Amazon's 'restrictive contracts' artificially inflate prices for consumers."

**为什么好：** 表达"价格上涨"时，多数学生只会用 `prices go up` 或 `prices increase`。`artificially inflate` 不仅精准传递了"被人为推高"的含义，而且`inflate`这个词本身比`increase`更有力度——它暗示了**不合理、非自然**的膨胀。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The new policy made housing prices go up. | The new policy **artificially inflated** housing prices. |
| Companies raise prices without reason. | Companies **artificially inflate** prices to maximize profits. |

> **例句：** Speculation in the real estate market has **artificially inflated** property values, making housing unaffordable for young people.

---

### 3. put … at an advantage over … 使……相对于……处于优势

**原文出处：** "Amazon wins because it controls pricing across the online retail sales market, putting itself at an advantage over everyone else."

**为什么好：** 写比较类作文时，学生常重复 `A is better than B`。`put A at an advantage over B` 不仅更正式，而且能清晰地表达**因果关系**——是某个因素（而不是泛泛的比较）让一方占优。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Technology makes this company better than others. | Technology **puts** this company **at an advantage over** its competitors. |
| Good education helps students do better. | Early exposure to English **puts** these students **at an advantage over** their peers. |

> **例句：** Bilingual education **puts** children from international schools **at an advantage over** those from regular public schools when applying to top universities.

---

### 4. have it exactly backwards 完全搞反了 / 本末倒置

**原文出处：** "The DC attorney-general has it exactly backwards — sellers set their own prices for the products they offer in our store."

**为什么好：** 这是议论文**反驳对方论点**的神器。多数学生反驳时只会写 `I don't agree` 或 `That's wrong`，语气生硬且缺乏力度。`have it exactly backwards` 不仅语气到位，而且自带一种"你们完全理解错了"的自信感。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| I don't think technology is making us lazy. | Critics **have it exactly backwards** — technology actually frees us to focus on creative work. |
| Some people think exams are useless. But they are wrong. | Those who dismiss exams **have it exactly backwards** — assessments drive learning motivation. |

> **例句：** Many parents believe that more homework leads to better grades. They **have it exactly backwards** — excessive homework often reduces learning efficiency and kills curiosity.

---

### 5. be seen as a test for … 被视为……的考验/试金石

**原文出处：** "The case will be seen as a test for whether other states can build their own cases against Amazon."

**为什么好：** 写预测性、展望性的结尾段落时，学生常用 `This will show whether...`。`be seen as a test for` 更书面化、更学术，而且自带**变被动为客观**的效果，适合用在议论文结尾段。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| This will show whether the new policy works. | This policy will **be seen as a test for** whether the government can balance economic growth and environmental protection. |
| This exam can tell if a student is good or not. | The college entrance exam **is seen as a test for** not only academic ability but also mental resilience. |

> **例句：** The upcoming climate summit will **be seen as a test for** whether the international community can move beyond empty promises and take real action.

## 写作建议：从"对抗性论证"学框架

这篇FT文章有个值得模仿的结构：**指控 → 证据 → 反方回应 → 结果判断**。这个四步法可以套用在几乎所有议论文中：

1. **指控（Claim）：** "Some argue that..." / "Critics have sued...for..."
2. **证据（Evidence）：** "They claim that this has artificially inflated..."
3. **反方回应（Rebuttal）：** "However, supporters have it exactly backwards..."
4. **结果判断（Judgment）：** "The debate will be seen as a test for..."

下次写议论文时，试试用这个四步框架，而不是"First, Second, Third"式地罗列观点。
