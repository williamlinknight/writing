## 本文要点

定语从句是英语写作中"描述力"的核心工具。一篇好的英文文章，从头到尾都在用定语从句来塑造人物、解释概念、连接观点。今天我们拆解《金融时报》对鲍里斯·约翰逊传记的书评，看看专业写作者如何用定语从句让文字鲜活。

---

## 一、FT原文精选——定从密集的"人物素描"

先看这段文字，短短一段话用了5个定语从句，把一个复杂人物立体地呈现出来：

> Boris Johnson is a perpetual paradox for admirers and opponents alike: **a loner who cannot bear to be by himself**; **a man of genuine intellect who still prefers to wing it**; a figure of ferocious ambition and great laziness; someone desperate to lead but unwilling to manage and mindless of consequences; ... **a man whose need to be loved in the moment explains almost all his moral flaws**.

再看另一处——描述父亲如何影响约翰逊的成长：

> **a neglectful, solipsistic and adulterous father who assaulted Johnson's mother**

以及非限制性定语从句的经典用法：

> Brenda Hale, president of the Supreme Court, **which ruled Johnson's prorogation of parliament to be unlawful**

还有省略关系代词的"接触从句"（contact clause）：

> **a man Bower calls an 'intelligent patriot'**

## 二、为什么好？——定语从句的四大功能

### 1. 用"who"把抽象标签变成具体形象

学生写作中常见的问题是形容词堆砌："He is a lazy and ambitious person." 但专业的写法是把形容词拆到定语从句里，每一个"who"后面跟着一个具体的动作或状态。

原文 `a man of genuine intellect who still prefers to wing it` 比简单说 "He is smart but lazy" 要好得多，因为"wing it"（即兴发挥）是一个具体场景，读者能直接想象出来。

### 2. 用"whose"表达"拥有关系"而不显笨拙

中文说"他的某个特质"，英文如果一直用"his..."会显得重复。`a man whose need to be loved in the moment explains almost all his moral flaws`——"whose"连接了一个完整的小故事：需要被爱→解释道德缺陷，因果逻辑自然嵌在从句里。

### 3. 用"which"引导非限制性定语从句补充背景信息

非限制性定语从句（前面有逗号）是在主句信息已经完整的情况下，额外添加解释。原文中 Brenda Hale 的身份已经明确，`which ruled Johnson's prorogation to be unlawful` 是补充背景。这种结构在议论文中特别有用，不会打断主句的推进。

### 4. 用省略关系代词的"接触从句"实现简洁

`a man Bower calls an 'intelligent patriot'`——这里关系代词"that"或"whom"被省略了，句式更紧凑、更口语化。这在文学性和叙述性文本中效果很好。

## 三、学生写法 vs 升级写法对照

| 场景 | 学生写法 | 升级写法（=定从） |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 描述人物矛盾性 | He is both smart and lazy. | He is **a man of genuine intellect who still prefers to wing it**. |
| 描述父亲影响 | His father was bad to him. | He had **a neglectful father who assaulted his mother**. |
| 描述人物性格成因 | He wants to be loved so he is not honest. | He is **a man whose need to be loved explains his moral flaws**. |
| 补充机构背景信息 | The Supreme Court ruled it was illegal. Brenda Hale is the president. | Brenda Hale, president of the Supreme Court, **which ruled the prorogation unlawful**, ... |
| 引用他人评价 | Bower calls him an intelligent patriot. | He is **a man Bower calls an "intelligent patriot"**. |

## 四、模板与例句

### 模板1：人物特质（who）

> **a/an [名词] who [动词短语]**

- 原文：a loner who cannot bear to be by himself
- 仿写：a leader who always puts his team before himself
- 仿写：a student who never settles for second best

### 模板2：人物缺陷来源（whose）

> **a/an [名词] whose [抽象名词] explains/drives/shapes [结果]**

- 原文：a man whose need to be loved explains almost all his moral flaws
- 仿写：a writer whose obsession with perfection drives her to rewrite endlessly
- 仿写：an athlete whose fear of failure pushes him beyond every limit

### 模板3：机构/事物补充（which）

> **[主句], which [动词 + 补充事实]**

- 原文：Brenda Hale, president of the Supreme Court, which ruled Johnson's prorogation to be unlawful
- 仿写：The school, which was founded in 1920, has produced three Nobel laureates
- 仿写：The policy, which critics say will hurt small businesses, was passed yesterday

### 模板4：紧凑评价（省略关系代词）

> **a/an [名词] [someone/something] calls [评价]**

- 原文：a man Bower calls an "intelligent patriot"
- 仿写：a strategy experts call "too risky to attempt"
- 仿写：a decision historians will remember as a turning point

## 五、练习：将以下句子升级为含定语从句的版本

1. He is a person. He never plans ahead and he always improvises.
2. Her father was very strict. Her father pushed her to succeed.
3. The new law was controversial. The new law banned single-use plastics.

> **参考答案：**
> 1. He is a person who never plans ahead and always improvises.
> 2. She had a strict father whose push for success shaped her entire childhood.
> 3. The new law, which banned single-use plastics, was highly controversial.
