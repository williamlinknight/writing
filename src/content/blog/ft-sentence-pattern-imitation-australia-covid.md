## 为什么学生作文像"流水账"？

很多学生写生活类作文，总是「First, I... Then, I... Finally, I...」三步走。句子短、结构平、没有亮点。

但好的英文叙事文，不是用连接词堆出来的——**而是用句型的变化制造节奏**。

今天我们从《金融时报》一篇关于澳大利亚疫情防控的个人叙事文中，提取 **3 个万能句型公式**。这篇文章由FT记者 Jamie Smyth 亲身经历写成，虽然讲的是疫情，但句型结构适用于任何生活类话题：一次经历、一个对比、一次观察。

## FT原文精选

> When I came down with a nasty head cold last week, my first concern was not Covid-19, but that I would miss coaching my son's team for their first game of the cricket season.
>
> My nonchalant reaction to developing symptoms reflects the phenomenal success Australia has achieved in suppressing the virus.
>
> Australians can now worry about relatively frivolous activities, such as attending kids' sport — and that is a luxury few people in Europe or the US currently enjoy.

## 句型一：「Not X, but Y」对比结构

**原文：**
> When I came down with a nasty head cold last week, my first concern was **not** Covid-19, **but** that I would miss coaching my son's team for their first game of the cricket season.

**结构公式：**
> When [事件] happened, [人物]'s first concern was **not** [A], **but** [B]

**为什么好：**
大多数学生写「I was worried about...」，直接陈述。而「not X, but Y」的结构制造了一个**转折期待**——读者以为你要说A，结果你在意的是B。这种「意外感」就是高级写作的起点。

**❌ 学生初版**
> When I got sick, I was worried about my exam.

**✅ 升级版**
> When I came down with a fever last week, my first concern was not the exam, but that I would miss my best friend's birthday party.

**💡 效果**
从「直述担忧」变成「对比两个担忧」，句子有了层次和故事感。

**模板：**
> When [event] happened, [person]'s first concern was not [A], but [B]

**例句迁移：**
- When I received the test results, my first concern was not the score itself, but whether I could still get into my dream university.
- When the company announced layoffs, employees' first concern was not the pay cut, but the loss of their colleagues.

---

## 句型二：复杂名词短语 + reflects

**原文：**
> **My nonchalant reaction to developing symptoms** reflects **the phenomenal success Australia has achieved in suppressing the virus**.

**结构公式：**
> [Possessive + adjective] reaction to [gerund/noun] reflects the [adjective] [noun] [entity] has achieved in [gerund/noun]

**为什么好：**
多数学生写「This shows that...」开头的简单句。而这个句型用一个**高度浓缩的名词短语**做主语（"my nonchalant reaction to developing symptoms"），后面用 reflects 连接一个名词性结论。主语本身就是一个小故事，信息密度极高。

**❌ 学生初版**
> My school has done a good job in teaching English. This shows our hard work.

**✅ 升级版**
> My school's remarkable progress in English education reflects the phenomenal effort our teachers have put into designing engaging lessons.

**💡 效果**
主语不再是简单的「I」或「My school」，而是一个有修饰语的名词短语，整句话立刻显得成熟。

**模板：**
> [Possessive + adjective] [noun] in [gerund] reflects the [adjective] [noun] [entity] has achieved in [gerund]

**例句迁移：**
- My parents' calm reaction to my disappointing exam results reflects the deep trust they have built in my ability to improve.
- The city's swift response to the natural disaster reflects the careful preparation the government has made in disaster prevention.

---

## 句型三：Dash + "that is a luxury" 情感收尾

**原文：**
> Australians can now worry about relatively frivolous activities, such as attending kids' sport — **and that is a luxury few people in Europe or the US currently enjoy.**

**结构公式：**
> [Subject] can now [verb] about [something], such as [example] — **and that is a [noun] few [people/places] currently [verb]**

**为什么好：**
这个句型的高级之处在于用**破折号后的对比**来制造情感冲击。前半句说「澳洲人可以操心一些小事」，后半句通过「that is a luxury few...currently enjoy」点出——这在欧美是奢侈。一个短句，两种视角，读起来很有力量。

**❌ 学生初版**
> We can now enjoy outdoor activities. But people in other countries cannot do this.

**✅ 升级版**
> Students in our school can now participate in outdoor sports clubs, such as hiking and cycling — and that is a luxury few young people in war-torn regions currently enjoy.

**💡 效果**
破折号让后半句有了「点睛」的效果，比用「but」连接更有冲击力。

**模板：**
> [Subject] can now [verb] about [something], such as [example] — and that is a [noun] few [people/places/entities] currently [verb]

**例句迁移：**
- Residents can now walk freely in the park, such as jogging or reading under the trees — and that is a privilege few people in crowded metropolises currently have.
- We can now focus on relatively trivial concerns, such as choosing what to eat for lunch — and that is a comfort few struggling families currently experience.

## 写作建议

这三个句型分别解决了叙事文写作的三大痛点：

1. **「Not X, but Y」** → 解决「开头太直白」
2. **「名词短语 + reflects」** → 解决「主语太单调」
3. **「Dash + that is a luxury」** → 解决「结尾太平淡」

**练习方法：**
- 第一周：每天写一个「Not X, but Y」句子，写一周
- 第二周：把句子的主语换成复杂名词短语
- 第三周：尝试在文章末尾用破折号结尾
- 把这三个句型抄在小卡片上，贴在书桌前

不要一次性全用在一篇文章里——**一篇用1-2个就够了，用对地方比用得多重要。**
