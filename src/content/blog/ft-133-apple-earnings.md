每次写"上升/下降"，你还在用 **increase / decrease** 吗？

这两个词没错，但英语写作里，精准的动词比泛泛的动词更能让阅卷老师眼前一亮。今天我们从FT一篇关于苹果季度财报的报道中，提取5个可以直接用在考场作文里的表达。

> Apple posted double-digit growth in the first quarter of 2021 in all of its businesses, led by **iPhone sales that surged by two-thirds** from a year ago.
>
> The world's most valuable publicly listed company said revenue **was up 54 per cent** in its fiscal quarter to $89.6bn, **far exceeding** already elevated forecasts. **Net profits jumped 110 per cent** to $23.6bn.
>
> **Mac sales grew 70 per cent** to $9.1bn and **iPad sales surged 79 per cent** to $7.8bn.
>
> **With cash continuing to pile up on its balance sheet**, Apple announced a $90bn share buyback programme.

> 苹果在2021年第一季度所有业务均实现两位数增长，其中iPhone销售额同比增长三分之二。
>
> 这家全球市值最高的上市公司表示营收增长54%至896亿美元，远超已经上调的预期。净利润飙升110%至236亿美元。

---

## 1. Surge — 猛增，比 increase 有冲击力

**原文出处：** *iPhone sales that surged by two-thirds from a year ago*
**中文解释：** surge 指"急剧上升、激增"，比 increase 更有画面感——像是潮水涌来一样。用在数据变化时，能立刻让读者感受到"增长幅度很大"。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The number of users increased. | The number of users **surged**. |
| Sales increased by 50%. | Sales **surged** by 50%. |

**模板：** `[Subject] surged by [number] per cent / to [value]`

**例句：** The popularity of online learning **surged** during the pandemic, attracting millions of new users.

---

## 2. Jump — 跳跃式增长，比 rise 更生动

**原文出处：** *Net profits jumped 110 per cent to $23.6bn*
**中文解释：** jump 字面意思是"跳"，用来描述数据时暗示"突然大幅增长"。它比 rise / grow 更口语化也更生动，适合用在议论文的论据段。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| The number increased sharply. | The figure **jumped** to a record high. |
| Profits rose quickly. | Profits **jumped** by more than half. |

**模板：** `[Subject] jumped [number] per cent / to [value]`

**例句：** After the new policy was introduced, the city's tourism revenue **jumped** by over 60%.

---

## 3. Far exceeding — 远超，分词结构的高级用法

**原文出处：** *far exceeding already elevated forecasts of $77bn*
**中文解释：** far exceeding 是现在分词短语作状语，相当于"which far exceeded"，但更简洁、更有"信息附加值"。学生作文里很少用分词结构，这是一个容易提分的点。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| …which was much higher than expected. | …**far exceeding** expectations. |
| The result was better than predicted. | The result was strong, **far exceeding** previous estimates. |

**模板：** `…, far exceeding [comparison baseline]`

**例句：** The company reported a 40% increase in revenue, **far exceeding** market analysts' predictions.

---

## 4. Thanks to / thanks in large part to — 自然引出原因

**原文出处：** *the past three quarters have been the biggest ever for the Mac, thanks in large part to the work-from-home trend*
**中文解释：** thanks to 比 because of 更地道，带一点"归功于"的积极语气。thanks in large part to 是更详细的版本，强调"很大程度上因为"。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Because of the new technology… | **Thanks to** the new technology… |
| This is because people work at home. | This is **thanks in large part to** remote work. |

**模板：** `[Result], thanks to / thanks in large part to [cause]`

**例句：** The school's exam pass rate improved dramatically, **thanks in large part to** the new writing-focused curriculum.

---

## 5. With + 名词 + 动词-ing — 独立主格结构，地道且高级

**原文出处：** *With cash continuing to pile up on its balance sheet, Apple announced a $90bn share buyback programme.*
**中文解释：** "With + 名词/代词 + 动词-ing" 是英语里非常常用的独立主格结构，表示"在……的情况下"。它在语法上独立于主句，但为动作提供背景。学生如果能用上，语法层次会立刻上一个台阶。

| 学生写法 | 升级写法 |
|---------|---------|
| Because there was more money… | **With** more money **piling up**… |
| As the deadline was approaching… | **With** the deadline **approaching**… |

**模板：** `With [noun] + [verb-ing], [main clause]`

**例句：** **With** the final exam **approaching**, many students began to review their notes more seriously.

**例句2：** **With** technology **developing** at an unprecedented pace, traditional industries are being forced to adapt.

---

## 写在最后

从外刊学表达，最重要的不是背单词，而是**看母语作者怎么把同一个意思用不同方式说出来**。

今天这5个表达——**surge, jump, far exceeding, thanks to, with...verb-ing**——全部可以直接用在考场作文里。下次写数据变化、分析原因的时候，试着用一用。
